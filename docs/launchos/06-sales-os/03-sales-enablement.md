already-canonical content elsewhere — this file is an index and usage guide,
not a new source of claims (Global Constraint 4: this module owns process
and ICP, not messaging, positioning, or product content).

## The one hard rule

**No claim gets improvised in a live conversation that isn't traceable to
one of the sources above.** This is the sales-conversation application of
Global Constraint 1 — a live conversation is higher-risk than written copy
because there's no review step before the words leave the room, which makes
pre-approved building blocks more important here, not less.

## What happens when a prospect asks something not covered above

If a prospect asks a question with no existing answer in any LaunchOS module
(a genuinely new question — not "what's your pricing" or "how does replay
work," which are covered):
1. Don't improvise a claim about the product, company, or commercial terms.
2. Say the honest thing: "I don't have that answer worked out yet — let me
   get back to you," or route to the standard disclaimer if it touches
   trading performance (the "Disclaimer present where required" checklist
   item, `01-brand-bible/05-usage-playbook.md`).
3. If the question recurs, that's a signal a new FAQ entry or a new module
   answer is needed — feed it back to Emad rather than let each
   conversation improvise its own answer independently (which would itself
   become a Global Constraint 4 violation over time — the same question
   answered differently in different sales conversations).

## Design-partner-specific framing

Since Stage 6 (`01-sales-process.md`) routes every qualified prospect toward
a design-partner relationship rather than an immediate sale, every stage's
content should be read through that lens: the goal of a sales conversation
today is not to close a commercial deal (none exists yet, per Module 05) but
to establish a design-partner relationship. Don't let demo or positioning
content accidentally imply a purchase decision is being requested — it isn't
yet.
