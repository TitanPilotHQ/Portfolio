# Positioning — 01. Category Positioning

## The category

**Supervised AI Trading** (Module 01, `01-brand-bible/01-identity.md`,
"Category"). Titan Pilot defines this category rather than borrowing an
existing one — the site's own copy positions it explicitly between two
adjacent, rejected categories:

- **Not an "AI trading bot"** — that category implies autonomous execution
  and, usually, implied performance promises. `COMPANY_PROBLEM`
  (`lib/content.ts:477-478`) explicitly rejects this: Titan Pilot "was not
  built to promise better returns or to create another black-box trading
  bot."
- **Not a "trading signal service"** — that category implies retail,
  low-accountability advice. `CONTACT_QUALIFICATION_NOTICE`
  (`lib/content.ts:571-572`) states this directly: "If you're looking for
  trading signals or investment advice, Titan Pilot is probably not the
  right fit."

## What sits where (the adjacent-category map)

|---|---|---|---|

| Trading signal service | Actionable tips for individual traders | Determinism, replayability, desk-level governance | Explicitly rejected — see qualification notice above |

| **Supervised AI Trading (Titan Pilot's category)** | A governed, replayable, evidence-backed record of AI's role in a trading decision — with deterministic risk gates as final authority | — | Defined by Titan Pilot |

## Why this category, not one of the existing ones

The four `WHY_CARDS` (`lib/content.ts:45-66`) state the category's actual
differentiating pillars, in the site's own words:

1. "Deterministic by Design" — "Every computable signal is generated by
   software, not guessed by a model."
2. "AI-Powered Insight" — "LLMs reason over structured market dossiers
   instead of raw candle noise."
3. "Risk First, Always" — "Capital protection, hard limits, and fail-closed
   behavior sit below every AI decision."
4. "Replayable Decisions" — "Each trade can be reconstructed from immutable
   events and verified after the fact."

No single existing category (bot, signal service, charting tool, no-code
automation, data terminal) combines all four. That combination — AI
reasoning + deterministic control + mandatory replay — is the category.

## Target segment (canonical definition lives in Module 06)

The target customer and ICP are canonically defined in Module 06 (Sales OS),
`06-sales-os/00-icp.md`, per Global Constraint 4 — not restated here. That
definition sharpens the category positioning: Supervised AI Trading is a
category for organizations that have already decided to use AI in trading
decisions and need to govern that use, not a category aimed at convincing
someone to start using AI trading tools for the first time. This is an
internal planning input, not yet a published site claim (Global Constraint 3
/ 1's grounding rule) — see `00-icp.md` for its status and sourcing.

## Category positioning statement (one paragraph, for internal/deck use)

> Supervised AI Trading is the category for organizations that already use
> AI to inform trading decisions and need to prove what it said, what
> evidence it used, how it was challenged, and who approved the final action.
> Unlike AI trading bots, it never gives AI execution authority. Unlike
> trading signal services, it isn't built for individual retail
> decision-making. Unlike charting tools, no-code automation platforms, or
> data terminals, it treats the AI-assisted decision itself — not the chart,
> the strategy, or the dataset — as the unit of record.
