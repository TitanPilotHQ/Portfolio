# Market Research Framework — 02. Market Research Scope

## The naming clarification

This module is named "Market Research Framework," per the master plan's
module list. That name could reasonably be read as "external research about
the trading-technology market" — its size, its growth rate, its competitive
landscape's revenue. That is not what this module currently contains.

The only sourced research content available to this program is Titan
Pilot's own technical/engineering research-*publication* program:
whitepapers, engineering reports, benchmark studies, and the other planned
publication types, documented in `01-research-doctrine.md` and sourced to
`lib/content.ts:517-563`. That is internal research Titan Pilot plans to
publish about its own engineering — not external research about the market
Titan Pilot operates in.

## No market-size figure exists here

No market-size figure — total addressable market (TAM), serviceable
addressable market (SAM), serviceable obtainable market (SOM), an
industry-revenue estimate, a market-growth rate, or a competitor
market-share percentage — exists in any source available to this program.

None is stated in this file, or anywhere else in LaunchOS, including:

- As a specific number.
- As a rough estimate or an order-of-magnitude range.
- As a "commonly cited" figure attributed to a third-party market-research
  firm. Citing an external firm's number would itself require a real,
  checkable source (the firm's report, a publication date, a methodology)
  that this program does not have. Gesturing at "analysts estimate the
  market at roughly..." without that source would be exactly the kind of
  fabrication the master plan's Quality bar prohibits — it does not become
  acceptable just because the number is attributed to someone else instead
  of invented outright.
- As an implied comparison or scale (e.g., relative to an adjacent,
  better-known market) that would let a reader back into a number this
  module isn't stating directly.

This module has zero market-size content to disclose because no such
research has been commissioned, produced, or sourced. That is the accurate
current state, not an oversight.

## This file's role: canonical future home, not a placeholder

If Titan Pilot ever commissions or produces external market-sizing
research, this file is the canonical location it will occupy — per the
master plan's Global Constraint 4 ("Research claims → the Research
repository / Market Research Framework (Module 15) only"). Establishing
that home now lets a future module or investor conversation cite one
canonical place for market-sizing facts rather than each module
inventing its own version.

This is deliberately *not* a `[TBD]` skeleton waiting to be filled with
placeholder numbers. Per the master plan's grounding rule, a module
requiring a fact that doesn't yet exist stays unfilled until the fact is
real and sourced — it does not get invented content in the meantime. This
section will only ever be filled in the same change that a real, sourced,
checkable market-sizing figure becomes available (see `03-governance.md`
for the exact trigger).

## Consistent with existing discipline elsewhere in LaunchOS

This module is not introducing a new instance of market-size avoidance —
the pattern is already true elsewhere in LaunchOS, without restating any
module's content:

- Module 04 (Positioning) documents category adjacency, the competitive
  map, and the positioning statement (`04-positioning/`) — all at the
  category and competitor level. It contains no market-size figure, though
  that follows from its scope rather than from any explicit stated policy
  against one (unlike Module 07, below). See that module for the actual
  content; it is cited here, not restated.
- Module 07 (Investor Data Room) explicitly lists "Market size or category
  adoption" under what its evidence is *not* — noting that market-sizing
  methodology belongs to this module once built, and that no number should
  be offered or invented until it is
  (`07-investor-data-room/02-traction-and-evidence.md`,
  `04-investor-faq.md`'s "total addressable market" answer). See those
  files for the actual content; it is cited here, not restated.

Both modules already avoid market-size claims. This module is where that
avoidance becomes explicit and permanent, and where the fact would land the
day it stops being an avoidance and starts being a sourced figure.
