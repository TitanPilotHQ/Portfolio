# Finance OS — 01. Financial Reality

## Revenue (Phase A — today)

> No revenue. Titan Pilot remains in the pre-revenue design-partner phase.

*(Source: `INTAKE-REQUEST.md` §6.1, Emad's direct answer, 2026-07-12, quoted
verbatim.)*

This is consistent with, and corroborated by, Module 05's canonical fact
that no customer is paying yet: "The current design-partner and
early-access phase remains free. No customer is paying yet."
(`05-pricing-system/01-pricing-philosophy.md`, sourcing `INTAKE-REQUEST.md`
§1.3). This file cites that fact rather than re-deriving or restating its
sourcing — Module 05 is its canonical owner.

## Funding and cost basis (Phase A — today)

> Financial management is currently founder-funded. Internal operating
> costs exist but are intentionally not documented publicly until
> incorporation.

*(Source: `INTAKE-REQUEST.md` §6.2, verbatim.)*

The "founder-funded" phrase here overlaps with Module 07's canonical
funding-raised fact: "No external funding has been raised. The company is
currently founder-funded." (`07-investor-data-room/03-cap-table-and-funding.md`,
sourcing `INTAKE-REQUEST.md` §3.1). This file cites Module 07 for that fact
rather than restating its sourcing. What intake §6.2 adds that Module 07
does not already state is specific to this module: internal operating
costs exist, and the decision has been made not to document them publicly
until incorporation.

## The distinction this file exists to protect

"Internal operating costs exist but are intentionally not documented
publicly" is not the same fact as "there are no costs." These are two
separate, independently true statements from §6.2:

1. Operating costs exist. This is stated as fact, not implied or inferred.
2. The size, composition, or amount of those costs is undisclosed by
   founder decision, pending incorporation.

Neither statement says or implies the costs are zero, small, large, or any
size at all. A reader should not fill that silence with an assumption —
this file does not, and no other LaunchOS module should either.

## What this file deliberately does not invent

- No dollar figure or currency amount of any kind, for any purpose
  (operating costs, funding, revenue, or otherwise).
- No percentage of any kind.
- No burn-rate estimate.
- No runway estimate (months of cash on hand, time-to-zero, or similar).
- No characterization of the undisclosed costs' size or nature — words like
  "modest," "lean," "minimal," "significant," or "material" would each
  assert something about a figure that isn't available. Stating plainly
  that no figure is available is the only accurate description; sizing the
  unknown, even with a hedge word, is not.
- No accounting method, tool, or record-keeping process detail — that is
  `02-accounting-and-reporting.md`'s subject, not this file's.

## Why disclosure is deferred to incorporation

Intake §6.2 ties non-disclosure to a specific trigger — incorporation —
without stating what will be disclosed at that point, in what form, to
whom, or on what timeline beyond "until incorporation." This file states
that conditional trigger because it is directly sourced; it does not
speculate about the shape of post-incorporation disclosure, since nothing
in the source describes it. The Phase A/B structure for what financial
*infrastructure* looks like once that trigger occurs — accounting method,
tooling, potential audit posture — is documented in
`02-accounting-and-reporting.md`, not duplicated here.
