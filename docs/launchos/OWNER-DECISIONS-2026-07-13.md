## §2 — "Formal engagement" threshold (Partnership OS)

> A partnership becomes a formal engagement only when at least one of
> these exists:
> - signed agreement
> - signed pilot or design-partner agreement
> - written letter of intent
> - paid engagement
> - jointly approved public announcement
> - formally scheduled technical integration with named owners and dates
>
> Informal calls, introductions and exploratory conversations do not
> count.

*(Source: Emad, direct decision, 2026-07-13, quoted verbatim.)* Canonical
location: `12-partnership-os/02-engagement-rules.md`.

**Follow-up clarification, same date** — the literal text above includes
"design-partner agreement" as a qualifying form, which risked conflating
this module's vendor/infrastructure-side "partnership" with Module 06/09's
customer-side "design partner." Asked to clarify; Emad's resolution:

> Choose: Add a clarifying note.
>
> Record the decision in Module 12 as:
>
> "A formal partnership engagement exists when there is a signed
> agreement, signed pilot, letter of intent, paid engagement, jointly
> approved public announcement, or formally scheduled technical
> integration with named owners and dates.
>
> A customer-side design-partner agreement governed by Modules 06 and 09
> does not, by itself, constitute a Partnership OS engagement.
>
> It falls within Module 12 only if the same counterparty also enters a
> distinct signed vendor, infrastructure, integration, distribution, data,
> cloud, brokerage, compliance, or other partnership arrangement."
>
> Also update the owner-decision register so the canonical meaning is:
> - design partner = customer-side validation relationship
> - partnership = vendor/infrastructure/distribution/integration
>   relationship
> - one counterparty may hold both roles, but only through separately
>   identifiable agreements or scopes
>
> Do not keep the ambiguous phrase "signed pilot or design-partner
> agreement" standing alone inside Module 12. Do not merge Modules 06/09
> and 12.

*(Source: Emad, direct decision, 2026-07-13, quoted verbatim.)*

## §3 — "Successful design-partner validation" threshold (Hiring OS)

> Do not use revenue alone.
>
> Validation requires:
> - at least one real design partner
> - completion of the agreed pilot period
> - regular product usage
> - no unresolved Critical safety or security issue
> - written confirmation that the product solves a meaningful operational
>   problem
> - at least one concrete workflow, product or commercial learning
>   incorporated into the roadmap
>
> This threshold becomes the hiring trigger only when workload can no
> longer be handled safely by the founder.

*(Source: Emad, direct decision, 2026-07-13, quoted verbatim.)* Canonical
location: `13-hiring-os/02-anticipated-hiring.md`. Note (added at PR #24's
adversarial review): intake §3.1 uses the identical phrase, undefined, for
Module 07's fundraising trigger — whether the same bar applies there is
not addressed by this decision; see `CLOSURE-REPORT.md` §5e.

**Follow-up clarification #2, same date** — three phrases ("active
pilot," "agreed pilot period," "agreed validation period") appeared across
§3 and §6 above without a stated relationship. Asked to clarify; Emad's
resolution:

> Use these canonical definitions:
>
> - Pilot period: the active product-use and workflow-testing window
>   during which the design partner uses Titan in the agreed environment.
> - Validation period: the broader design-partner evaluation window,
>   beginning with onboarding and including the pilot period, evidence
>   collection, feedback, issue resolution and formal closeout review.
>
> Therefore:
> - "active pilot" and "agreed pilot period" refer to the same active-use
>   phase.
> - "validation period" is broader and may extend before and after the
>   active pilot.
> - weekly feedback applies during onboarding and the active pilot.
> - monthly executive review applies throughout the validation period
>   where duration warrants it.
> - formal closeout occurs at the end of the validation period.
> - successful design-partner validation is assessed only after the pilot
>   is complete and the validation closeout has occurred.
>
> Standardize all affected documents to these terms and add one canonical
> glossary definition rather than repeating competing definitions.

*(Source: Emad, direct decision, 2026-07-13, quoted verbatim.)* Canonical
glossary location:
`09-customer-success-os/03-success-and-production-readiness.md`.
